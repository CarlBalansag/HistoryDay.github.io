Nuclear Bombing

In 1945 United States introduces Nuclear Weapons into the world by dropping 2 nuclear bombs in Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to Japan surrender and Ending World War 2.

(Troutman)

(Hulton)






In 1939 the U.S President Franklin Roosevelt approved the establishment of nuclear weapons development, known as the Manhattan Project.(Fenton)




They discussed with Scientifc Panel and the Interim Comittee and planned the nuclear bombs to be used against Japan as soon as possible, also to be dropped surrounded by adjacent houses and building most susceptible to damage and to not have prior warning.(Stimson)

(Department of Energy)

(Los Alamos National Laboratory)





On 8:15 am on August 6th 1945 the first atomic weapon “Little Boy” ever was released over a human population above Hiroshima Japan.(Fenton) “Little Boy” delivered 12.5 kilotons of TNT expanding 5 square miles of the city centre to ashes.(The Atomic Bomb That Ended The Second World War)







By the end of 1945 the estimated killed in the bombing in Hirsohima is 140,000 people, and remaining survivors would obtain leukemia, cancer and other side effects from the radiation.(Hiroshima and Nagasaki Bombing)

Three days later at 11 am, the second weapon was originally planned to be detonated on the city Kokura where a Japanese army base is located, but was delivered to Nagaski instead because there were heavy clouds over Kokur.(Fenton)



The bomb that detnated over Nagasaki was called “Fat Man”, 2 squares miles of the city were pulverised.(The Atomic Bomb That Ended The Second World War)

The blaze leveled part of the city resulting in 80,000 deaths and approximately destroying 30% of the city's population which additionally caused casualty later due to radiation exposure.(Vaughn)





On August 10, the Japanese government indicated that it would accept defeat on certain conditions. August 14th Japan finally accepted unconditional surrender, the following day was declared “Victory over Japan '', It was not until September 2nd where Japanese surrender was signed.(The Atomic Bomb That Ended The Second World War)

The effects lasted decades, people suffered from increasing rates of leukaemia to the surviving survivors and survivors suffered from cancers at a high rate. Pregnant women had higher rates of miscarriage and deaths among their infants.(Hiroshima and Nagasaki bombing)










The bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki started the arms race between two superpowers to amass the most powerful nuclear weapon with the most effective delivery time leading towards the cold war.(Reasons for the cold war)
